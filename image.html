Garden Dahlia

Dahlias are flowers known for their look, as well as their meaning. In the victorian era, dahlias symbolized commitment and everlasting union. For this reason, dahlias are commonly used in weddings. Relatives of the dahlia include sunflowers, daisies, chrysanthemums, and zinnias. Dahlias typically bloom in the spring and are unscented. However, they attact pollinators through their vibrant colors.

Colors

White, pink, yellow, purple, red, black, green, and multicolored.

Region of Origin

Mexico and Central America
