Garden Dahlia

Dahlias are flowers known for their look, as well as their meaning. In the victorian era, dahlias symbolized commitment and everlasting union. For this reason, dahlias are commonly used in weddings. Dahlias typically bloom in the spring and are unscented. However, they attract pollinators through their vibrant colors.

Relatives

Sunflowers, daisies, chrysanthemums, and zinnias.

Colors

White, pink, yellow, purple, red, black, green, and multicolored.

Region of Origin

Mexico and Central America
